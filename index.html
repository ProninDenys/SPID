About the Project

SpaceID provides decentralized access to space mission datasets, enabling innovation in science, research and open data systems.

The Problem

Publicly-funded space missions produce vast amounts of valuable data, but this information is difficult to access, scattered across agencies, or locked behind restrictions. Innovation stalls when data is hidden.

The Solution

SpaceID creates a unified, blockchain-powered protocol for structured, permissionless access to historical and live mission data. Powered by SPID token, it enables indexing, staking, and global participation.

Tokenomics

FAQ

Common questions about the SpaceID platform.

How do I buy SPID?

SPID will be available on selected launchpads and later on decentralized exchanges. Stay tuned to our updates.

Is SPID a governance token?

Yes, SPID holders will vote on key protocol decisions, including data access models and network upgrades.

What data can I access?

You can access satellite imagery, telemetry, scientific readings and archived mission reports.

Do I need to stake SPID to access data?

Some datasets are free; others require staking or holding SPID depending on size, bandwidth, or recency.

How secure is the platform?

All data is verified on-chain. Smart contracts are audited and stored redundantly via IPFS and nodes.
